published in 2014, *make it stick: the science of successful learning* synthesizes decades of cognitive-science research on memory and learning. the authors argue that the methods that *feel* most productive (re-reading, highlighting, massed practice) produce the weakest retention, while harder, slower techniques (retrieval practice, spaced repetition, interleaving, elaboration) build lasting knowledge.
